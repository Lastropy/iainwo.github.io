### Review of Last Weeks Goals
Last week I had several goals - related to both study and life, they were these:

Life goals:
1. Be in bed, phone away from my bed, by or before 10 PM
2. Ensure to plan the next day's schedule on my Calendar before going to sleep

Study goals:
1. Finish module #5 of Coursera's Math for ML: Linear Algebra
2. Finish module #1 and #2 of Coursera's Math for ML: Multivariate Calculus
3. Read Least Linear Squares chapter of ThinkStats

I am very happy to say I succeeded with my Study Goals #1 and #2 - they out of all of last weeks Goals were the most important. I also accomplished my Life Goal #1 - get to bed by 10 PM (except for one day - where my disciplined slipped and I spent too much time trying to figure out this algorithm question).

I failed when it came to 1) making sure to have my schedule planned and 2) reading the Least Linear Squares chapter of ThinkStats. The second goal early into the week I realized I wasn't going to hit and quickly dropped that goal. I came to the conclusion that it was a "nice-to-have" but not a prerequisite - also it didn't factor into the mid-term goals I laid out above.

The 1) not planning out my schedule the night before was a critical failure - I should have had that done. Maybe 2 or 3 days of the week I forgot to do it the night before. To be honest I was so wiped that I once I finished grinding through whatever I had at the end of the day - I quickly took care of hygiene related things: teeth, shower, laundry and then fell straight to sleep.

I like planning my schedule at the end of the day because it is most apparent to me what I need to work on during the following day - but also it helps me set my mind for what I need to do the following day. It's as much practical as it is a mental exercise pour moi. I think going into the week I am going to continue to keep that activity around the time I go to bed and make sure to remind myself - via calendar, or by doing it half-hour earlier in that night so I don't just skip past it.

Going into next week my life goals are:

2. Plan calendar night before

I found that I functioned better with more sleep (i.e getting to bed at 10 PM.) this week I want to try to get to bed earlier - starting at 9:30 PM.
